    Definitions

    A zero hour contract is a type of contract between a worker and employer, where the employer is not legally obliged to provide any minimum working hours, and the worker is not legally obliged to accept any hours offered. For this indicator, data on employment in zero-hours contracts are being used as a proxy for ‘informal employment’ (‘informal employment’ is defined in the UN global indicator metadata).

    This indicator is being used as an approximation of the UN SDG Indicator. Where possible, we will work to identify or develop UK data to meet the global indicator specification. This indicator has not been identified in collaboration with topic experts.
